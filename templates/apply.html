What is NSFAS?

The National Student Financial Aid Scheme — free funding for qualifying students

NSFAS provides financial assistance to students from low-income families to study at public universities and TVET colleges in South Africa. If your household earns R350,000 or less per year, you may qualify for full funding covering tuition, accommodation, meals, transport and learning materials.

How to Apply for NSFAS

Follow these steps carefully — apply early for best results

1

Check Eligibility

Household income must be R350,000/year or less. Must be a South African citizen. Must have a valid SA ID number.

2

Register on MyNSFAS

Go to my.nsfas.org.za and create your account using your ID number and a valid email address.

3

Complete the Application

Fill in all personal details, household income information, and institution choice accurately.

4

Upload Documents

Attach all required documents clearly scanned or photographed. Missing documents = delays.

5

Submit & Track

Submit your application and track the status on myNSFAS. Check regularly for updates or requests.

6

Accept the Award

If approved, log in to myNSFAS and accept your funding award. Register at your institution.

NSFAS Application Timeline

Typical annual cycle — dates may shift, apply as soon as applications open

September – October: Applications Open

NSFAS opens the application portal for the following academic year. Apply immediately — do not wait!

October – November: Matric Exams

Complete your Grade 12 exams while your NSFAS application is being processed.

January: Results & Provisional Funding

Check myNSFAS for your outcome. Provisionally funded students can register at university.

February – March: University Registration

Register at your institution. Inform the financial aid office of your NSFAS application.

March onwards: Allowances Disbursed

Monthly allowances are loaded onto your NSFAS wallet card or bank account.

How to Apply to a University

General steps — always check the specific university's website for exact requirements

1

Research Your Options

Choose 2–3 universities and identify your preferred course. Check APS requirements for each.

2

Check APS Score

Calculate your Admission Point Score using your Grade 11 and 12 results. Use our APS calculator tab.

3

Complete Online Application

Apply on each university's official website. Most applications open in April–September for the following year.

4

Pay Application Fee

Most universities charge R100–R200 application fee. Keep your proof of payment.

5

Submit Documents

Upload certified ID, Grade 11/12 results, proof of residence, and other required documents.

6

Wait for Offer Letter

Provisional acceptance letters arrive November–January. Apply for NSFAS at the same time.
