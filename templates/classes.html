🎯 NBT Test Prep

National Benchmark Tests (NBT)

Required by many South African universities for admission. It tests your Academic Literacy, Quantitative Literacy, and Mathematics — results are used alongside your APS score to assess university readiness.

3
Tests
3½h
Duration
R200
Approx. Fee

What is the NBT?

📋

Who must write it?

All students applying to universities that require the NBT. Check your chosen university's website to confirm.

📅

When do you write?

Tests are written at official NBTP venues around SA. Registration opens from June. You can write multiple times.

📊

How are results used?

Results are classified as Proficient, Intermediate, or Basic. Some faculties use them to place you in extended programmes.

🌐

How to register?

Register at nbt.ac.za. You need your ID, matric number, and payment. Apply early — venues fill up fast!

The 3 NBT Tests

📖 Academic Literacy (AL)

Academic Literacy

Tests your ability to understand and use written language for academic study. Focuses on comprehension and reasoning — not grammar rules in isolation.

Reading comprehension Vocabulary in context Text structure Inference & reasoning Understanding graphs Academic conventions

⏱ ~3 hours (combined with QL)  |  🌍 English or Afrikaans

📄 Official Sample 🌐 nbt.ac.za
📐 Mathematics (MAT)

Mathematics

Tests mathematical knowledge needed for demanding programmes. Based on your Grade 12 Maths syllabus — strong matric maths = strong NBT MAT.

Algebra & functions Number systems Geometry & trigonometry Data & probability Patterns & sequences Basic calculus

⏱ ~3 hours (separate sitting)  |  📌 Required for: Engineering, BSc, Actuarial Science

📄 Official Sample 🌐 nbt.ac.za
📊 Quantitative Literacy (QL)

Quantitative Literacy

Tests ability to manage and interpret quantitative information in everyday contexts. More applied and practical than the Maths test.

Percentages & ratios Reading graphs & tables Number relationships Spatial reasoning Financial calculations Estimation

⏱ ~3 hours (combined with AL)  |  📌 Required for most university applications

📄 Official Sample 🌐 nbt.ac.za

💡 Top NBT Preparation Tips

1

Start Early

Begin NBT prep at least 3 months before your test. Last-minute cramming doesn't work for skills-based tests.

2

Use Official Samples

Do the official NBTP sample tests under timed conditions to understand exactly what to expect.

3

Read Every Day

Academic Literacy improves through regular reading — articles, editorials, academic texts, not just social media.

4

Revise Grade 12 Maths

The MAT test is curriculum-based. Strong Grade 12 maths revision is your best MAT preparation strategy.

5

Practise with Data

For QL, practise reading graphs and tables. Work with real-life percentages, ratios and financial scenarios.

6

Register on Time

NBT venues fill up! Register as soon as registration opens and choose a venue close to home.
